<h3>The Golden Ginko</h3>
<p>The ginkgo (<em>Ginkgo biloba</em>)is a medium-sized tree native to the Orient, but widely planted in the Southestern US.The ginkgo is considered a &ldquo;living fossil,&rdquo;in that there are no closely related speciesstill extant. Ginkgos are prized for their vivid yellow fall leaves.<img style="float: right;" src="http://ginkopic.jpg" width="172 align=" height="220" /></p>
<p>An unusual biological feature of the ginkgo is that it is a gymnosperm (&ldquo;naked seed&rdquo;), meaning that the ginkgo seed is not encased in a fruit. In this the ginkgo resembles pine trees, although the ginkgo is deciduous (loses its leaves in the winter).</p>
<p>Ginkgos can be extremely long lived; one ginkgo in Shandong province, China is estimated to be 3,000 years old.&nbsp;</p>
